# Content Quality & E-E-A-T Analysis: webot.agency

The site is a high-converting, high-signal landing page. It avoids generic AI fluff in favor of "proof of work" and transparent pricing, which creates strong trust.

## Content Metrics

### 1. Word Count & Coverage
- **Page Type:** Homepage
- **Minimum Required:** 500 words
- **Estimated Count:** ~1,050 words
- **Status:** ✅ Satisfied. The page provides comprehensive coverage of the offering, process, and proof without unnecessary padding.

### 2. Readability & Tone
- **Readability:** High. Employs a "brutalist" copywriting style—short sentences, active voice, and high contrast.
- **Tone:** Direct, confident, and "no-nonsense." It differentiates itself from "AI consultants" by emphasizing building over talking.

### 3. Keyword Optimization
- **Primary Keywords:** *Custom AI Agents, Automation Systems, AI Agent Development*.
- **Analysis:** Keywords are integrated naturally into headers and service descriptions. There is no evidence of keyword stuffing; the focus is on intent and value proposition.

## AI Citation Readiness

**Score: 82/100**

- **Structured Data:** Minimal identified in HTML (no explicit JSON-LD for `Service` or `Organization` found in the main body).
- **Quotable Facts:** High. Specific metrics (e.g., "580+ files, 1,700+ searchable chunks") are prime for LLM extraction and citations.
- **Hierarchy:** Excellent. Logical flow from Value Prop $\rightarrow$ Products $\rightarrow$ Services $\rightarrow$ Proof $\rightarrow$ FAQ.

## Freshness & Quality Signals

- **Update Signals:** Copyright date is 2026 (future-dated/current). "Coming Soon" tags on products indicate active development.
- **AI Content Markers:** Low. The copy avoids typical AI tropes (e.g., "Unlock your potential," "In the rapidly evolving landscape"). The voice is distinct and personalized.

---

## Improvement Recommendations

1. **Implement Schema.org Markup:** Add `Service` and `Organization` JSON-LD to explicitly tell search engines exactly what is offered and who the provider is.
2. **Add External Validation:** While "dogfooding" is a great experience signal, adding a few third-party testimonials or links to public GitHub repositories for some of the "skills" would boost Authoritativeness.
3. **Expand "Our Work" into Case Studies:** The current proof is concise. Transitioning those summaries into dedicated case study pages with "Problem $\rightarrow$ Solution $\rightarrow$ Result" would significantly increase the "Experience" and "Expertise" signals for deeper SEO.
4. **Privacy/Terms Pages:** The footer links to Privacy and Terms are currently placeholders (`#`). Implementing these is a prerequisite for full "Trustworthiness" in the eyes of Google's Quality Raters.
